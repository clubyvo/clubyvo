Cova Santa is one of Ibiza's best-kept secrets — a truly unique venue that feels nothing like a conventional nightclub. Set in the pine-scented Ibizan countryside near San José, it is built around a natural cave discovered in the 15th century and opened to the public in 1957. Today, as part of the Amnesia Group, it has evolved into one of the island's most atmospheric and celebrated venues, combining a world-class restaurant, a breathtaking open-air amphitheatre and an intimate indoor cave dancefloor in a single magical location.

An evening at Cova Santa is a journey, not just a night out. It begins in the open-air garden as the sun sets over the Ibizan hills, continues through the restaurant with its spectacular views, and transitions after midnight into the cave itself — dark, intimate and entirely focused on music. Rather than a superclub experience, Cova Santa offers something rarer: a bohemian, sensory night that feels deeply rooted in the island's original spirit. Residencies for 2026 include the legendary Woomoon, PIV (Tuesdays), Guy Gerber (Wednesdays), GALA (Thursdays — a surrealist-inspired art, music and performance ritual) and The Zoo Project.
