FAQ — Nightlife in Barcelona

What are the best nightclubs in Barcelona? ▾
The top clubs include Opium Barcelona (Passeig Marítim 34), Shôko (ranked #7 in the world), Razzmatazz (5 rooms, Poblenou), Pacha Barcelona (Port Olímpic), Sala Apolo & Nitsa (Poble Sec), INPUT (Funktion-One system), Moog and Macarena (underground techno) and Otto Zutz (Zona Alta).
What time do clubs open in Barcelona? ▾
Most Barcelona clubs open around midnight but don't get busy until 2–3am. Closing times are typically 5–6am. Arriving before 1:30am is rarely necessary — the city runs very late.
What are the best areas for nightlife in Barcelona? ▾
Barceloneta and Port Olímpic for beachfront clubs (Opium, Shôko, Pacha), Poble Sec for underground and live music (Sala Apolo, Nitsa), El Raval and the Gothic Quarter for intimate techno spots (Moog, Macarena), Poblenou for large venues (Razzmatazz), and Zona Alta for selective upscale clubs (Otto Zutz).
How much does entry cost in Barcelona clubs? ▾
Entry prices range from free or €5 at smaller venues like Macarena (before 2am) to €8–20 at Moog and Razzmatazz, up to €20–25+ at premium beachfront venues. Many clubs offer discounted or free entry via guest list on Instagram or official apps.
Are Barcelona clubs open all year? ▾
Yes — unlike coastal resorts, Barcelona clubs are open year-round. Most venues run Thursday through Sunday, with some (like Macarena and Moog) open seven nights a week. Summer brings extra events and outdoor programming.
