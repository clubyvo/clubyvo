Lloret de Mar Nightlife Guide

Lloret de Mar's club scene is almost entirely concentrated in a few blocks around Carrer de la Salle and the beachfront Passeig Mossèn Jacint Verdaguer. Unlike cities where clubs are spread across different neighbourhoods, in Lloret you can walk between five major clubs in under ten minutes.

The crowd is predominantly 18–25 and international — you'll meet British, German, Italian and Scandinavian holidaymakers on any given night. Music leans heavily toward commercial EDM and Reggaeton, though some venues run hip-hop and R&B nights.

When to go

Peak season (July–August) is the most intense — every club is open, queues can be long, and the atmosphere is electric. June and September offer a slightly more relaxed experience with shorter queues and the same clubs open.

Outside of summer (October–May), the vast majority of Lloret clubs are closed or operate only on weekends with reduced capacity. If you're planning a winter trip specifically for the nightlife, consider a different destination.

Tips for clubbing in Lloret

Pre-drink on the strip: The bars along Carrer de la Salle offer cheap drinks and a warm-up atmosphere before clubs open at midnight.

Guest lists: Most clubs offer free or discounted entry before 1am via their Instagram or website guest lists. Worth checking before you go.

Dress code: Lloret is casual — trainers and shorts are fine at most venues. Only the more upscale beach clubs have stricter door policies.

FAQ — Clubs in Lloret de Mar

What are the best clubs in Lloret de Mar?
According to clubyvo reviews, the best clubs in Lloret de Mar are Tropics Club (9.0), Colossos (8.7), and Disco Palace (8.5). All three are open nightly during the summer season.
When is the best time to visit Lloret de Mar for nightlife?
The peak season runs from late June to early September. July and August offer the full experience with all clubs open nightly. For a less crowded but still lively trip, the first two weeks of June or the last two weeks of September are ideal.
How much does it cost to get into clubs in Lloret?
Entry prices are generally very affordable compared to Ibiza or other European destinations — typically €10 to €20, often including a drink. Many clubs also offer free entry before 1am or with a guest list.
Is Lloret de Mar good for a club holiday?
Yes — Lloret is one of the most affordable and accessible club holiday destinations in Europe, particularly popular with younger crowds. Everything is within walking distance, prices are low, and the summer atmosphere is consistently high-energy.
Are there foam parties in Lloret de Mar?
Yes — foam parties are a Lloret de Mar staple. Clubs like Euphoria and Colossos regularly host foam nights during the summer season. Check their social media for specific dates.
