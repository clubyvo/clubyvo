Milan Nightlife Guide

Milan's club scene is split across several distinct districts, each with its own character. The Navigli canal area is best for aperitivo and bar crawls before midnight, while the real clubs cluster around Via Pietrasanta and the Porta Romana / Porta Vittoria area — Milan's answer to Berlin's warehouse district.

The city has a strong underground techno tradition, with clubs like Amnesia Milano and Volt consistently booking the same international names you'd find at Berghain or fabric. Unlike London or Berlin, Milan clubs rarely open before midnight — don't show up before 1am.

Best areas for nightlife in Milan

Navigli: Canal-side bars and aperitivo spots. Good for early evenings and more casual nights out. Less techno, more mixed crowd.

Porta Romana / Via Pietrasanta: Milan's industrial club corridor. Home to Volt, Magazzini Generali and the harder underground sound.

Isola: Hip neighbourhood with boutique venues and a creative crowd. Good cocktail bars and intimate club nights.

Centro / Corso Como: More commercial and luxury-oriented. Think bottle service, dress codes, and EDM.

FAQ — Nightlife in Milan

What are the best nightclubs in Milan?
The top-rated clubs in Milan on clubyvo are Amnesia Milano (9.2), Volt Club (8.9), and Plastic Milano (8.7), all ranked by thousands of verified reviews from real clubbers.
What time do clubs open in Milan?
Milan clubs typically open at 11pm–midnight but don't fill up until 1–2am. Most close between 5am and 6am. Arriving before 1am is generally not necessary and may result in a nearly empty venue.
How much does entry cost at Milan clubs?
Entry prices in Milan range from €10 for smaller underground clubs to €25–30 for larger commercial venues. Many clubs offer free or discounted entry before a certain time or with a guest list signup.
What music do clubs in Milan play?
Milan has a strong techno and house scene, particularly in the warehouse district around Via Pietrasanta. You'll also find hip-hop, R&B and commercial EDM nights at larger venues like Magazzini Generali and Tocqueville 13.
Are there open air clubs in Milan?
Yes — during summer (June–September) several Milan venues host outdoor parties and rooftop nights. Some clubs also move to temporary outdoor locations on the city's outskirts for festival-style events.
